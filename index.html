AP Computer Science Principles

At RePublic High School, computer science instruction is a core tenet of our scolars' academic profile. We have led an AP For All program over the last 5 years. In 2020, over 70% of our 10th grade scholars passed the exam, and our scholars outperformed the state of Tennessee and globally.

Women of Color in Computer Science

Women of color are vastly underrepresented in the tech industry. Organizations such as Kode With Klossy and NCWIT have created programming to support young women in STEM that are our scholars have participated in. At RHS, we are also particularly committed to changing this narrative. Our AP-For-All program is a two-time recipient of the College Board's Female Diversity Award - a recognition given to schools that are increasing the number of female-identifying students enrolled in AP Computer Science.

  • Learn More

Building a Pipeline with Pivot Tech

At RePublic, we are building a community partnership with Pivot Technology School - a local tech bootcamp committed to increasing the number of underrepresented minorities in the field of tech. Students enrolled in this program will graduate with a digital portfolio of projects and an industry-recognized certification in front-end web development, which will open more job opportunities for our students and help them thrive in the local Nashville economy.

My Pathway Into Tech

I am interested in the intersection of entrepreneurship, technology, and education. I am fascinated by the market opportunity within the American school system, and at the same time, I recognize the deeply human and empathetic nature of education. I am excited by the potential that edtech companies can create a more equitable future within classrooms.
